Abstract

The Theophysics framework rests on 188 axioms spanning seven domains: information theory, quantum mechanics, grace/sign algebra, consciousness, morality, destiny, and cosmology. This paper demonstrates that these 188 axioms are not an arbitrary list but the unpacked contents of a single generating function — the Lowe Coherence Lagrangian (LLC). Each of the LLC's ten variables compresses an entire domain. When one variable is isolated and the others held fixed, the LLC reduces to that domain's natural equation: Shannon entropy for information, Schrödinger for quantum mechanics, sign algebra for grace, integrated information for consciousness, the coherence functional for morality, the sign attractor for destiny, and modified Friedmann for cosmology. The compression chain — 188 axioms → 7 domains → 33 cross-domain axioms → 8 schemata → 1 equation — is lossless. The prism metaphor captures the structure: white light (the LLC) enters a prism and reveals seven colors (domain equations) that were always inside it. The domain equations are not analogies or parallels — they are projections of the same generating function onto different coordinate systems. When all variables are active simultaneously, the LLC produces a single terminus: χ = 𝒞 = Christ.

When my daughter was little, I bought her one of those triangular pieces of glass you can hold up to a window. White light goes in one side. On the other side, every color in the rainbow comes out. She asked me where the colors came from. I told her they were always in the light. The glass just separates them so you can see what was already there. She thought about that for a second. Then she said: so the rainbow was hiding in the white?

The Theophysics framework began with 188 axioms. That number isn't arbitrary — it reflects the actual scope of the claim. If you're going to argue that physics, theology, information theory, consciousness, morality, cosmology, and eschatology all point to the same underlying structure, you need axioms in all of those domains. 188 is what honest coverage looks like.

But 188 axioms have a problem: nobody can hold 188 things in their head at once. You can't present them. You can't argue from them. You can't defend them in a conversation. The framework was true in the way a dictionary is true — technically complete, practically unusable. So we compressed. And what we found was that the compression wasn't losing information. It was finding the coordinate system that the information was always organized around.
